Early Life & Education

Before becoming a giant of Reformed theology, Warfield was a young man of science. Growing up in Kentucky, he was deeply interested in livestock breeding and mathematics. He graduated from Princeton University in 1871 and, after a brief stint studying in Europe, felt the call to the ministry.

His unique background in rigorous scientific observation heavily influenced his later theological work, leading him to treat the study of Scripture with the precision of a scientist and the heart of a believer.

Notable Works

The Inspiration and Authority of the Bible

His definitive defense of biblical inerrancy, arguing that the Bible is both fully human and fully divine.

Counterfeit Miracles

A rigorous historical critique of modern miracles, defending the "Cessationist" position.

Studies in Tertullian and Augustine

An exploration of the historical giants who shaped the Latin Church's understanding of grace.

Enter the Archives

Warfield’s life was a masterclass in balancing intellectual rigor with personal sacrifice. Beyond the books, he spent nearly 40 years caring for his wife, Annie, rarely leaving Princeton so he could be by her side.
